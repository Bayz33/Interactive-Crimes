Case 10: The Father’s Day Murder

VERY HARD

During a Father’s Day barbecue at the luxurious Hollow Creek Estate, wealthy businessman Richard Lawson collapsed face-first beside the grill.

At first, investigators believed it was a heart attack — until toxicology found a deadly sedative mixed into his whiskey.

Every family member had a motive: inheritance disputes, hidden affairs, and years of resentment. But only one person had the perfect opportunity.

Your mission:

  • Who murdered Richard Lawson?
  • What was the motive?
  • What key evidence proves it?

Witness Statements

Emily Lawson (Daughter): “Dad barely touched alcohol lately. But someone handed him a whiskey right before dinner.”

Trevor Mills (Neighbor): “I saw Richard arguing with his mother-in-law near the patio about money.”

Sarah Lawson (Wife): “My mother hated Richard, but she stayed inside most of the evening.”

Daniel Pierce (Family Friend): “Richard received a Father’s Day card with no name on it. He looked disturbed after reading it.”

Grill Chef: “Only family members were near the drink table before dinner.”

Evidence Collected

Toxicology Report: A strong veterinary sedative was found in Richard’s whiskey glass.

Security Camera: Footage shows Richard’s mother-in-law near the drink table minutes before the toast.

Father’s Day Card: The unsigned card read: “You ruined this family long before today.”

Financial Records: Richard recently cut his mother-in-law out of his estate after discovering stolen money.

Kitchen Trash: Investigators found an empty veterinary sedative bottle wrapped in barbecue napkins.
