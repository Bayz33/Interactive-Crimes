Case 1: The Ember Lounge Arson

On June 1st, just after closing time, The Ember Lounge went up in flames. When fire crews broke in, they found the charred remains of Marcus Kent, the bar’s manager.

Arson investigators ruled it suspicious. Five people were seen at the bar that night. One of them is lying.

Your mission: Solve the case by answering three questions:

  • Who started the fire?
  • What was their motive?
  • What key evidence proves it?

Crime Scene Report

Location: The Ember Lounge – Downtown nightlife district.

Fire Origin: Behind the bar, near the top-shelf liquor display.

Estimated Ignition Time: 1:08 a.m.

Victim: Marcus Kent, 37. Burned remains were found near the rear exit, keys fused in his hand.

Investigator’s Note: “Accelerant was likely used. Origin area shows heavy charring. Suggests intentional ignition.”

Witness Statements

Sasha Morgan (Bartender): “We closed out around 12:45. Marcus stayed to finish the books. He was edgy. Said something about double-checking the locks. I left at 12:50. He told me he'd handle the rest alone.”

Jared Lin (Musician): “I was hauling my guitar case out. Saw Marcus arguing with some guy in a hoodie outside the back door. Couldn’t hear what they said, but it was heated. Then I left. That's all I know.”

Bianca Dale (Regular): “I was in the restroom. When the lights flickered, I smelled smoke. I panicked and ran out without seeing anyone. I didn't even grab my purse.”

Chris Hall (Ex-Employee): “Look, I was just there for my final paycheck. Marcus refused to face me. I never even went inside. I left when he ignored me. We hated each other, but I'm not a killer.”

Maya Ruiz (Waitress): “I stayed to help sweep up. Marcus was tense. Kept checking over his shoulder. He told me he thought someone was following him. I left around 12:50. He seemed spooked.”

Evidence Locker

Bar Tab Printout: Printed at 12:46 a.m. Shows an unpaid tab under Chris Hall’s name, totaling $182. Marcus circled it and wrote: “Settle this.”

Security Still: Grainy image from rear entrance camera. Captured at 1:02 a.m. Shows a hooded figure slipping through the back door. Features obscured.

Recovered Phone: Melted remains of Marcus’s phone. Forensic recovery extracted one undelivered text, timestamped 1:03 a.m.: “He’s back. If anything happens, it’s him.”
