Case 4: The Abandoned Asylum Files

An old asylum sits rotting on the edge of town. Police abandoned the investigation decades ago. Now a new lead emerges, suggesting the deaths weren’t suicides—but murders covered up by someone on staff. Can you prove it?

Your mission: Solve the case by answering three critical questions:

  • Who committed the crimes?
  • What was their motive?
  • What key piece of evidence proves it?

Crime Scene Report

Location: Old Fairview Asylum – abandoned psychiatric facility.

Discovery: Human remains in basement cells. Records show multiple deaths listed as suicides.

Investigator Note: "Strangely consistent hanging method. Suspicious injuries suggest restraint before death."

Archived Statements

Nurse Caroline: “Dr. Morse said these patients were violent. Said restraints and isolation were for everyone’s safety.”

Janitor Ed: “Heard screams at night. Morse would stay late alone. He paid me to forget what I heard.”

Former Patient (letter): “She lied on my diagnosis to keep me locked up. I’m not insane. She wanted us for experiments.”

Evidence Locker

Forged Patient File: Diagnosis altered to read "dangerously psychotic." Real notes underneath indicate stable mental health.

Recovered Confession Note: Found in Morse’s office after her disappearance. “I did what had to be done. No one will understand.”
