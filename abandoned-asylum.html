Case 4: The Abandoned Asylum

FREE for a limited time

Once a bustling psychiatric hospital, now only rotting walls and old legends. But the truth is hidden in what remains. Decades ago, a patient vanished without a trace. The case went cold. Until now.

Your mission: Solve the case by answering three critical questions:

  • Who is responsible for the disappearance?
  • What was their motive?
  • What key piece of evidence proves it?

Crime Scene Report

Location: Eastgate Asylum – abandoned for 20 years. Crumbling hallways, collapsed roofs, mold-eaten records.

Evidence Found: Patient file with missing pages. Blood-stained bedsheet. Staff logbook with redactions.

Investigator’s Note: “Records are damaged but some staff logs hint at an internal cover-up.”

Historical Witness Statements

Dr. Evelyn Shaw (Psychiatrist): “Director Harris always acted like he knew more than he said. He had keys to everything.”

Orderly Tom Price: “They moved Patient 14 at night with no paperwork. I heard arguing. Then silence.”

Nurse Linda Coe: “Files went missing after Director Harris did his so-called ‘audit.’ He claimed it was to protect us from scandal.”

Evidence Locker

Staff Logbook: Redacted pages. Entries stop on the night of Patient 14’s disappearance.

Blood-Stained Bedsheet: DNA testing inconclusive due to age and contamination.

Partial Patient File: Missing history. Notes read “uncooperative,” “violent,” “requires transfer.”
