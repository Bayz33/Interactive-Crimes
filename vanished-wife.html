Case 3: The Vanished Wife

On November 3rd, Sarah Smith was reported missing by her husband. He claimed she left to visit family but never arrived. Her car was found abandoned outside town. Neighbors heard arguments the night before. Evidence suggests an abduction—but is it really?

Your mission: Solve the case by answering three critical questions:

  • Who abducted Sarah?
  • What was their motive?
  • What key piece of evidence proves it?

Crime Scene Report

Location: Suburban neighborhood on the edge of town.

Vehicle: Sarah's car found unlocked, purse inside, keys missing.

Timeline: Last seen arguing with husband late at night. Reported missing the next morning.

Investigator's Note: "No signs of forced entry or struggle in car. Possible voluntary departure staged as abduction."

Witness Statements

Neighbor: “Heard raised voices around midnight. Thought it was just a fight. Didn’t see anyone leave.”

Friend: “Sarah said she was scared of John. He was acting strange about money lately.”

John Smith (Husband): “I don’t know why she left. We were fine. Maybe she just needed space.”

Evidence Locker

Insurance Policy: Newly updated life insurance listing John as sole beneficiary.

Motel Receipt: Paid in cash the day before under John's initials.

Financial Records: Large withdrawal made from joint account the day she vanished.
