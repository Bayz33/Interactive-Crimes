Case 3: The Vanished Wife

On a quiet suburban street, Jenna Morris vanished from her home one evening without a trace. No signs of forced entry. No witnesses saw her leave. Police suspect foul play. Her husband insists he found the door ajar when he returned.

Your mission: Solve the case by answering three critical questions:

  • Who planned the abduction?
  • What was their motive?
  • What key piece of evidence proves it?

Crime Scene Report

Location: 218 Willow Lane – single-family home.

Time of Disappearance: Between 8:00 p.m. and 9:00 p.m.

Entry: No forced entry; door found slightly open.

House Status: Clean except for knocked-over lamp.

Investigator’s Note: "No forced entry suggests victim knew the abductor. Minor signs of struggle indicate it was quick."

Witness Statements

Greg Morris (Husband): “I came home late from work. Door was open. Jenna was gone. Lamp was on the floor.”

Emily Fields (Neighbor): “I heard shouting around 8. It sounded like Jenna. I didn’t see anything.”

Marcus Grant (Friend): “Greg was venting to me about money troubles. But he said they were working it out.”

Detective Rhodes: “Greg kept changing his story. First said he left at 7, then 7:30.”

Evidence Locker

Phone Call Log: Greg called Jenna three times between 8:00–8:10 p.m. despite saying he was "driving."

Life Insurance Policy: Recently increased to $500,000.

Jenna’s Diary Entry: “He’s been so angry lately. I’m scared of what he might do.”
