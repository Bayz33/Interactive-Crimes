Case 5: The Wrongly Accused

A homeless man named Samuel Price is on death row for murder. The evidence seems airtight: blood on his clothes, no alibi, weapon found in his camp. But the more you dig, the more the story unravels. Conflicting witnesses, bribery, and a landlord with everything to gain. Can you see what the jury missed?

Your mission: Solve the case by answering three critical questions:

  • Who actually committed the murder?
  • What was their motive?
  • What key piece of evidence proves it?

Crime Scene Report

Location: Vacant apartment building, urban alley entrance.

Scene Details: Victim stabbed in 2nd-floor hall. Blood trail leads outside toward the camp Samuel Price stayed at. Knife recovered in dumpster near his tent.

Investigator’s Note: “Case looked open-and-shut. But missing security footage and landlord's sealed records suggest something darker.”

Witness Statements

Marcy Dawes (Neighbor 1): “Samuel threatened me once. Said he’d burn my house down.”

Leo Marks (Neighbor 2): “The landlord, Jacob Hart, was furious about the camp. He said he’d do anything to get them out.”

Security Contractor: “The cameras were 'offline.' Landlord told us to skip that month's backup.”

Nadine Reyes (Tenant): “Jacob Hart offered me $200 to say I saw Samuel fighting with the victim. I didn’t take it.”

Samuel Price (Defendant): “I didn’t kill anyone. I found the knife in the trash. The landlord was always threatening us.”

Evidence Locker

Insurance Policy: Pays landlord $500,000 if building condemned due to violent crime.

Unsigned Threat Letter: “GET OUT OR ELSE” found in the victim’s room. Handwriting inconclusive.

Security Log: Shows footage marked “corrupted” for the night of the murder.

Bribery Ledger: Landlord's withdrawals match witness payment offers.

Knife Analysis: Samuel’s prints but no blood on handle. Blood wiped clean suggests planting.
