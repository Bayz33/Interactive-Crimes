Case 5: Wrongly Accused

A man sits behind bars for a murder he didn’t commit—or so he claims. As new evidence emerges, it’s up to you to reopen the case, sort truth from lies, and bring justice where the system failed.

Your mission: Solve the case by answering three critical questions:

  • Who actually committed the crime?
  • What was their motive?
  • What key evidence proves it?

Crime Scene Report

Location: Lincoln Apartments – Unit 4B. Blood spatter on the wall. Blunt force trauma.

Victim: Emily Carson, 29. Found dead in her apartment. Neighbor called in a disturbance.

Original Conviction: Devon Marks, boyfriend, sentenced on circumstantial evidence and witness testimony.

Reopen Trigger: Anonymous letter sent to public defender’s office: “You got the wrong man.”

Witness Statements

Neighbor Tina Lopez: “I heard shouting. A man’s voice. Thought it was the boyfriend again. He always sounded angry.”

Building Maintenance – Ron Dillon: “I saw a man in a hoodie leave around 11. Didn’t look like Devon. Slimmer. Faster walk.”

Public Defender Elise Tran: “The timeline never sat right with me. Devon was seen on traffic cam ten blocks away around 10:55.”

Evidence Locker

  • Anonymous Letter: “You got the wrong man. She knew who really did it.”
  • Surveillance Footage: Blurry footage shows unknown man entering 4B at 10:47 p.m. wearing a hoodie.
  • Voice Mail: Emily left a voicemail for a friend: “He said he’d kill me if I told. I don’t know what to do.”
