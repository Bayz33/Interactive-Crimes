The Wrongly Accused – Master Investigator (Very Hard)

Case 5: The Wrongly Accused

A homeless man named Samuel Price is on death row for murder. The evidence seemed airtight: blood on his clothes, no alibi. But something feels off. The property owner benefited hugely from the insurance payout. Can you prove Samuel's innocence and unmask the real killer?

Your mission: Solve the case by answering three critical questions:

  • Who actually committed the murder?
  • What was their motive?
  • What key piece of evidence proves it?

Crime Scene Report

Location: Vacant apartment building downtown. Scene of a violent stabbing.

Initial Findings: Blood trail leading to Samuel Price's camp. Knife found near his belongings.

Investigator's Note: "Open-and-shut, they said. But the security system was 'offline.' Landlord refuses to share financial records."

Witness Statements

Neighbor (Mrs. Dawes): “Samuel would ask for leftovers. He was harmless. But the landlord threatened to ‘clean up’ the block.”

Security Contractor: “The cameras were off. Landlord told us to skip that month's maintenance.”

Samuel Price (Defendant): “I didn’t kill anyone. I found the knife in the trash. It wasn’t mine.”

Evidence Locker

Insurance Policy: Landlord stands to gain $500,000 on 'vandalism and loss of tenants' clause if property condemned.

Eviction Notice Draft: Unsent letter threatening Samuel if he didn't leave the block.

Knife Analysis: Samuel's fingerprints but no blood on handle. Suggests it was planted.
