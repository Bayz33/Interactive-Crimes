Case 5: The Wrongly Accused

Three years ago, Nathan Cole—a homeless man—was convicted of the murder of local shop owner Gerald Myers. He claimed innocence. The case seemed closed: a bloody knife in his shopping cart, an eyewitness placing him near the scene. But new inconsistencies have surfaced.

Your mission: Reinvestigate this conviction by answering three critical questions:

  • Who actually committed the murder?
  • What was their motive?
  • What key piece of evidence proves it?

Crime Scene Report

Location: Myers Grocery, a small neighborhood store, closed at 11 p.m. The body was found near the register, multiple stab wounds.

Evidence Collected: Bloodied knife recovered in Nathan's cart. Store was ransacked but money left behind. Partial fingerprints on the back door.

Investigator’s Note: “Scene suggested a struggle. No forced entry at the front. Back door was unlatched.”

Witness Statements

Officer Laura Grant: “Cole was picked up blocks away with the knife in his cart. He seemed confused, possibly drunk. Didn’t resist arrest.”

Leo Marks (Neighbor): “I heard arguing. Then quiet. A back door slam. Didn’t see anyone clearly.”

Anthony Myers (Victim’s Son): “Dad mentioned threatening to evict a camp behind the store. Said they were scaring customers.”

Evidence Locker

Knife: Bloody, partial prints too smudged for ID. Cole insists he “found it” in a trash bin.

Store Camera: Offline for three hours. Unknown cause. Cables were damaged behind shelving.

Back Door Prints: Partial, do not match Cole. APD didn’t pursue new matches after conviction.
