Case 2: The Jewelry Store Heist

On September 12th, the upscale Midtown Jewelers was robbed just before closing. The owner was tied up in the back and a tray of rare diamonds was stolen. Investigators found no sign of forced entry. Four employees were working that night. One of them planned it.

Your mission: Solve the case by answering three critical questions:

  • Who planned the heist?
  • What was their motive?
  • What key piece of evidence proves it?

Crime Scene Report

Location: Midtown Jewelers – luxury retail store downtown.

Entry: Front door, no forced entry. Alarm was disarmed.

Time of Robbery: Estimated between 8:45–8:55 p.m.

Owner Statement: Tied up with zip ties in the back. Heard one voice giving orders.

Investigator’s Note: "Alarm code was used correctly. Likely an inside job. No fingerprints on the diamond tray, suggesting gloves."

Witness Statements

Amy Chen (Sales Associate): “We were about to close. I was in the back grabbing cleaning supplies. Heard the commotion and hid.”

Carlos Rivera (Security Guard): “Patrolled as normal. Cameras went out for 5 minutes. When they came back, everyone was gone.”

Lena Price (Manager): “I was counting the register. Carlos came to check in. Everything was fine. Then suddenly it went dark.”

Derek Moore (Jeweler): “I stayed late to finish setting a ring. I heard shouting. I bolted. I didn't see anything.”

Evidence Locker

Alarm Log: Disarmed at 8:42 p.m. using Lena’s unique code.

Security Footage: Shows Carlos at his desk. System cuts out at 8:43 p.m.

Recovered Note: Crumpled note in the trash: "Meet after close. Split 50/50."
